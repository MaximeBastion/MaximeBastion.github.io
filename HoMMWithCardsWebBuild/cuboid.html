Cuboid - Project Overview

Introduction


This project is a Unity remake of the game Cuboid.

Cuboid is a 3D puzzle game where the player controls a rectangular parallelepiped, the Cuboid, and moves it on a grid.

Work in progress. The project will be over and fully covered on this website by June, 8th.

Goal


To complete a level, the player needs to be standing on the exit block.

He needs to find the shortest correct sequence of movements to reach it, avoid falling and to interact with the level via switches.

Defining a map


A map is defined by 3 layers.

Each layer is a two-dimensional array of integers.

Each integer represents a particular item.

For instance, a 1 in the layer 1 array represents a regular block.


  • - Layer 1: The grid

    The layer 1 contains the grid of the map. It defines where the player can move.

    Layer 1 blocks are the regular, weak, and bridge blocks.

    If the Cuboid stands on a weak block, it will break and the player will fall.

    A bridge block can be either on or off. The player will fall if on top of an off bridge.

  • - Layer 2: Interactables

    In the layer 2 we place switches the player can interact with.

    Regular and strong switches toggle the presence of one or several bridge blocks.

    Strong switches require the player to be standing on to be activated.

    Teleportation switches will separate the Cuboid in two parts, and teleport them somewhere on the map.

    To complete the level, the player needs to merge the two part together before falling into the exit block.

  • - Layer 3: Targets

    This layer is not visible by the player.

    It's a two-dimensional array of integers which associates items together, such as switches with bridge blocks.

Level sequence


In the level sequence menu, the player can create the sequence of levels he will play with.

He can also toggle the use of timers.

If the timer is activated, he will have a time limit for each level of the sequence.

If he times out, he will restart the sequence.

Level Editor


In the level editor, the player can build is own levels.

He can choose blocks in the first two layers, and assign targets when in the third layer.
