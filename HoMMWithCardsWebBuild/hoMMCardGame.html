HoMM Card Game - Project Overview

Introduction


HoMM Card Game is a crossover between the combat system of Heroes of Might and Magic and a card game like Magic: The Gathering.

The goal for me during this project is to practice my Unity scripting skills, and not art.

Work in progress.

Goal


The goal of each player is to destroy the opponent’s Altar located on the opposite side of the board.

He can do so by hitting it with units he summons and by casting spells.

He must prevent his opponent from destroying his altar as well.

Mana


In order to play cards, a player needs mana. Players start with 0 mana, and they gain each turn a number of mana equal to the current turn’s number.

Unused mana is conserved.

Cards


There are two types of cards:

  • - Unit cards:

    When played, unit cards allow the player to summon a unit on a cell of is choice (only on the first row for the first player, and on the last for the second player).

    On the card, we can read in order the movement range, attack range, mana cost, attack damage and health.

  • - Spell cards:

    When played, they apply an immediate effect on units and/or players. Some spells can affect multiple targets, while others can affect only one.

    On the card we only read the mana cost.

Units


Units are creatures players summon in order to attack the opponent’s altar and protect their own.

They have a mana cost, an attack damage, a starting health, a movement range and an attack range value.


Every turn except for the turn it has been played, a unit can perform one or both of its actions in any order:

  • - Moving:

    The unit can move to a target cell if it’s distance to him is inferior or equal to its movement range value, and if it’s empty.

  • - Attacking

    The unit can attack a target unit or enemy altar if the target belongs to an opponent and if it’s distance to it is inferior or equal to its attack range value.

    It then deals damage to it equal to his damage value.

    If its health goes to 0 or less, the unit is removed from the battlefield, or in case of an altar, the active player wins the game.

Zones


The zones system is highly inspired by Magic: The Gathering.


A card is always in a zone, and there are three zones for each player:

  • - The Library:

    When the game begins, every zone is empty, except for the libraries, which contain the deck of each player (the pile of 40 cards they play with this match).

    Players draw a card from their library at the beginning of each of their turn.

  • - The Hand:

    When a player draws a card, it goes in its hand. A player can only play cards from his hand.

  • - The Graveyard

    When a spell card is played or a unit dies, it goes into its owner's graveyard.
